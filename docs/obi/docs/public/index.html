OBI Documentation

OBI is an ontological and Bayesian reasoning infrastructure for uncertainty-aware robotic and humanitarian systems.

What is OBI?

OBI means Ontological Bayesian Intelligence. It is an ontological and Bayesian reasoning infrastructure for uncertainty-aware robotic and humanitarian systems.

Its documentation focuses on semantic graphs, Bayesian inference, stochastic models, uncertainty, and reasoning infrastructure for real-world assistance systems.

Why uncertainty matters

Human-facing and robotic systems rarely receive complete information. OBI treats incomplete evidence, conflicting signals, and probabilistic confidence as first-class inputs instead of forcing every question into a brittle yes/no state.

Ontologies as semantic graphs

Ontologies are used as structured semantic graphs: nodes represent concepts, states, events, or actors, while edges describe relationships and interpretations. Coloring, partitioning, and labeling can expose different semantic roles inside the same knowledge structure.

Bayesian reasoning

Bayesian reasoning gives OBI a way to update belief under evidence. Probabilities are not decorative metadata; they express how strongly the system can support a claim given available observations and prior structure.

Stochastic subject matter

Some subject matter varies over time, context, or observer. OBI preserves this stochastic character so the system can normalize, compare, and reason over distributions without pretending uncertain material is perfectly stable.

Top-down and bottom-up reasoning

Top-down reasoning starts from a model, ontology, or hypothesis and tests observations against it. Bottom-up reasoning starts from evidence and builds toward structure. OBI needs both directions because real systems must diagnose, explain, and adapt.

Semantic drift and data drift

Data drift changes observed distributions. Semantic drift changes the meaning or interpretation of concepts. OBI documentation keeps both visible because robust reasoning has to notice when evidence changes and when the language around evidence changes.

Filter/flash reasoning cycle

The filter/flash cycle separates careful constraint checking from rapid hypothesis formation. A filter phase can remove unsafe or unsupported interpretations, while a flash phase can propose candidate explanations for further validation.

OBI for robotics

For robotics, OBI frames sensors, commands, environment state, and safety checks as uncertainty-aware reasoning inputs. The goal is infrastructure that helps machines act with contextual awareness while remaining inspectable by humans.

Humanitarian real-world systems

OBI is oriented toward assistance systems that operate in messy real conditions: healthcare, safety-critical environments, accessibility, field support, and other humanitarian settings where uncertainty and ethics cannot be ignored.

What OBI is not

OBI should not be described as conscious AI. It is not a claim of machine consciousness, sentience, or personhood. It is a reasoning infrastructure for uncertainty-aware robotic and humanitarian systems.

Glossary

Ontology
A structured representation of concepts and relationships in a domain.
Bayesian inference
A method for updating belief when new evidence appears.
Semantic graph
A graph whose nodes and edges carry interpretable meaning.
Stochastic
Having probabilistic variation that can be modeled but not predicted perfectly.
Data drift
A change in the observed data distribution over time.
Semantic drift
A change in the meaning or usage of concepts over time or context.
